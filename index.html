Nuclear Weapons Glossary

Kiloton (kt)

Explosive yield equivalent to 1,000 tons of TNT. Used to measure smaller nuclear weapons.

Megaton (Mt)

Explosive yield equivalent to 1 million tons of TNT. Used for strategic thermonuclear weapons.

Air Burst

Detonation above ground level to maximize blast damage area while minimizing fallout.

Surface Burst

Ground-level detonation that creates a crater and significant radioactive fallout.

Overpressure

Blast wave pressure measured in PSI. 5 PSI collapses buildings, 1 PSI shatters windows.

Thermal Radiation

Intense heat and light that causes burns and fires. Can blind or burn exposed skin miles away.

Fireball

Superheated sphere of gas at detonation point. Everything inside is vaporized instantly.

Fallout

Radioactive particles that fall from the mushroom cloud, contaminating large areas downwind.

EMP

Electromagnetic pulse that damages electronics. High-altitude bursts can affect entire regions.

Ground Zero

The point on Earth's surface directly below an air burst or at the center of a surface burst.

CEP

Circular Error Probable - the radius within which a missile has a 50% chance of landing.

MIRV

Multiple Independently-targetable Reentry Vehicle. One missile carrying multiple warheads.
